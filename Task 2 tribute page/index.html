Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

▶

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was intimately involved in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India." A leader of vision and action, Dr. Kalam inspired millions with his life and work. His passion for science and education led him to be a beloved figure in India and around the world.

Achievements

  • SLV-III: Directed the project that developed India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) in 1980.
  • Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP): Led the initiative that developed missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
  • Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: Played a pivotal role in the nuclear tests conducted in 1998.
  • Vision 2020: Formulated a plan to transform India into a developed nation by 2020.
  • Teaching and Mentorship: Passionately engaged in teaching and inspiring young minds.
